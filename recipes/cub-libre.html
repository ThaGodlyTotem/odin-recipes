Cuba Libre Recipe

Description

The drink was created in Cuba in the early 1900s, but its exact origins are not certain. It became popular shortly after 1900, when bottled Coca-Cola was first imported into Cuba from the United States. Its origin is associated with the heavy U.S. presence in Cuba following the Spanish–American War of 1898; the drink's traditional name, "Cuba libre" (Free Cuba), was the slogan of the Cuban independence movement.

Ingredients

  • 2 fluid ounces rum
  • ½ fluid ounce lime juice
  • ¾ cup ice
  • 2 fluid ounces cola-flavored carbonated beverage

Directions

Combine rum and lime juice in a highball glass. Top with ice and fill with cola.
