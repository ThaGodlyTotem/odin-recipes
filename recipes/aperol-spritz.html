Aperol® Spritz Recipe

Description

A spritz is a Venetian wine-based cocktail, commonly served as an aperitif in Northeast Italy. It consists of prosecco, digestive bitters and soda water. The original Spritz Veneziano (Italian pronunciation: [ˈsprits venetˈtsjaːno]) uses Select as bitters and was created in Venice in 1920. Popular variants are spritz al Campari which uses Campari and Aperol spritz which uses Aperol as bitters.

Ingredients

  • 4 ice cubes, or as desired
  • 2 fluid ounces Prosecco
  • 1 splash carbonated water
  • 1 (1.5 fluid ounce) jigger bitter orange aperitif (such as Aperol®)
  • 1 slice orange

Directions

Fill a wine glass with ice cubes; pour in Prosecco and carbonated water.
Top cocktail with bitter orange aperitif. Garnish with orange slice
